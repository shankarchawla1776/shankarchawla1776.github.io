My Current Favorite Integral

My current favorite integral is
$$ \int^{\infty}_{-\infty} \frac{e^{-x}}{(1+e^{-x})^2} \text{d}x = \int^{\infty}_{-\infty} \frac{\text{d}}{\text{d}x}\left( \frac{1}{1+e^{-x}}\right) \text{d}x = \int^{\infty}_{-\infty} \frac{\text{d}}{\text{d}x} \sigma(x) \text{d}x = 1$$
where $\sigma(x)$ is the logistic function. The improper integral converges to 1, which is cool both because it shows that the derivative of the logistic function is a probability distribution (specifically, $\sigma'(x)$ is the PDF of the logistic distribution), and that the logistic function is a sigmoid function, meaning that the total vertical change is 1.
